The Monty Hall Problem

Welcome! This is a classic problem in probability and decision theory. Named for the host of the television game show "Let's Make a Deal," Monty Hall. See the Monty Hall Problem page on Wikipedia for more information.

In this game, you will choose one of three doors. Behind one door is a car, and behind the other two are goats. After you make your choice, the host will open one of the other doors to reveal a goat. You will then have the option to stick with your original choice or switch to the other unopened door.

Will you win the car by sticking with your choice or by switching? Let's find out!

Statistics

According to the laws of probability, you have a better chance of winning the car if you switch doors after the host reveals a goat.
